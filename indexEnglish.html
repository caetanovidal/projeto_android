Android Mascot History

You probably know that the Android, operating system maintained by Google is one of the most used for for mobile devices in the world. BUt maybe you don't know that your mascot has a very curious name and history? Well, follow this article to lear a lot about this little robot.

The first version

The first attempt to create a mascot came in 2007 and came from a developer called Dan Morrill. He says he opened Inkscape (open software for vectorization of images) and create his own version of a robot. The goal was just to personify the system just for his team, there was no request from the company to create a mascot.

This first bizarre version was even named in honor of its creator: it would be the Dandroids.

A new mascot emerge

The idea of ​​having a mascot matured and the mission was passed on to a professional in the area. The Russian illustrator Irina Blok, also a Google employee, was given the mission to represent the little robot in a more pleasant way.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything graphically with few strokes and in a flatter way. The drawing should also generate quick identification with the viewer. Then came Bugdroid, the new Android mascot.

The main inspiration for the features of the new Bugdroid came from those little dolls that illustrate bathroom doors to indicate the gender of each door. Legend has it that the artist was creating on her desk in the Google and looked towards the bathrooms and the identification was immediate: simple, clean, objective.

Want to learn more?

Another curious thing about Android is that each version has always been named after a candy, in alphabetical order from version 1.5 to 9.0.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 3.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lolipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, Android Qdid not exist, as Google decided to put an end to this fun practice and started using numbers, which gave rise to Android 10.

Access the website here Android History to know the sequence of the "sweetened" versions and what each one brought to the Android system.
